Requirements

Every fall parents and students ask the Graphic Design faculty for advice on what type of equipment to purchase for use in our program. Below is our recommendation.

Equipment and software in our program is based entirely on the Apple Macintosh platform, we recommend Apple products for our students

What screen size is the best? Do I need more storage space? Do I need an additional hard drive? Depending on your financial ability the answers to these questions will depend. If you have any questions please contact Bill Rees, program director.

The best thing to do is visit an Apple store and test the equipment there before making your decision.

Apple Laptops:

The Graphic Design program requires each student to have a MacBook pro. Your computer will have to run the latest operating system for the next 4 years. MacBook Pro models from 2013 and newer can run the latest version of macOS. This cut off year will increase each year.

If you currently have a MacBook Pro newer then 2017 this device should support the newest operation systems over the next 4 years.

To see a list of currently supported MacBook Pros you can visit Apple's support page.

Mice and Keyboards

A Bluetooth mouse and keyboard are required. Our design labs have monitors for you to connect your laptop to, and it's much easier to use a keyboard and mouse when doing design work.

Faculty picks:

While Apple products are a premium price, we have found that the durability and longevity of these devices far out last their cheaper competitors.

iPads

Due to the ever changing demands of our course work and the graphic design field, an iPad is becoming more and more useful. The iPad is not a replacement to a desktop or laptop in our field but another tool to further develop and showcase your work and ideas. iPad recommendations can be provided when asked.

Digital Camera

A digital camera is required, but not until your sophomore year. Please wait to purchase any equipment until that time. Your photography professor will have specific requirements concerning a camera.
